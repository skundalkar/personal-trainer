# Personas

## Maya — consistency seeker

- Wants: short, approachable sessions at home and encouragement to return after a missed week.
- Constraints: limited equipment, limited time, dislikes aggressive fitness messaging.
- Product response: 10–20 minute recommendations, gentle tone, completion-focused progress, and optional music.

## Jordan — gym learner

- Wants: to understand which exercises make sense for a selected muscle group and how to set them up safely.
- Constraints: has basic equipment but is unsure about exercise selection and camera angles.
- Product response: transparent “why this” ranking, equipment alternatives, and camera setup/fidelity gates.

## Alex — experienced but time-constrained

- Wants: quick, effective sessions that use known preferences and clear progressions.
- Constraints: no patience for lengthy onboarding or vague motivation.
- Product response: concise tone, saved defaults, efficient alternatives, and a compact session plan.

## Accessibility principle

People vary in movement capacity, camera comfort, body shape, and training goals. The product must allow users to exclude exercises, reduce intensity, disable camera use, and choose language/tone without being penalized by recommendations.
