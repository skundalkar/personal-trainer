# Product Decisions Log

| Decision | Initial direction | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Core value | Personal, explainable recommendations plus limited form coaching | Recommendations create retention; camera coaching earns trust only when conservative and validated |
| Efficiency | Goal-specific fit, not a universal ranking | “Best” depends on the person, goal, equipment, time, and adherence |
| Personalization | Explicit preferences plus behavior signals | Ratings and completion are more respectful and useful than hidden inferences |
| Motivation | Supportive, user-controlled, non-shaming | Encouragement should help people return, not create pressure |
| Camera | Local-first, opt-in, confidence-aware | Video is sensitive and 2D camera estimates have real limits |
| Music | Spotify first; YouTube link-out as a fallback | Avoids unauthorized music playback and keeps integrations optional |

## Open decisions

1. Is the first audience primarily at-home beginners, gym beginners, or mixed?
2. Should the first goal be general fitness, strength, hypertrophy, or habit-building?
3. Which music experience matters most: choosing an existing playlist, mood recommendations, or playback control?
4. Should a qualified trainer review exercise rule packs before they appear in the app?
